<p><ul><li>Prostaglandins</li><li>Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitor</li><li>Cholinergic Drugs</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Open Angle Glaucoma drugs market comprises several types, including prostaglandins, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, cholinergic drugs, and others. Prostaglandins work by increasing aqueous humor outflow, effectively lowering intraocular pressure. Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors reduce fluid production in the eye. Cholinergic drugs enhance fluid drainage through the trabecular meshwork. Other drug categories may include beta-blockers and alpha agonists, each offering distinct mechanisms for managing glaucoma. Together, these treatments aim to preserve vision by controlling eye pressure effectively.</p></p>
